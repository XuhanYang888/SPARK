General Questions

What does SPARK actually do?
SPARK empowers high school and college students to lead engaging, hands-on STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Math) workshops for elementary and middle school students, especially in communities where access to hands-on STEM activities is limited. Branch leaders recruit passionate volunteers to bring these experiences to life.
What does the Headquarters do?
The SPARK Headquarters is a team of dedicated students who support branches across the globe. They create lesson plans, record educational videos, manage the website, and provide financial and logistical support to local branches when needed.
How much does it cost to start a branch?
Nothing at all! All SPARK resources—curriculum, training, support—are completely free. There’s no cost to get started. We do offer optional merchandise, but it’s not required. For us at HQ, the greatest reward is seeing branches make a lasting impact.
What is the origin of this organization?
SPARK began as “Kids Are Scientists Too” (KAST) over 15 years ago. It started with a few passionate individuals and quickly grew to over 20 regions, led by high school and undergraduate students bringing STEAM to young learners. When KAST HQ ceased operations in 2021, many branches chose to continue independently, uniting under a new name—SPARK—to keep the mission alive and growing.
How can I help?
The best way to help? Start a SPARK branch in your community—especially if your school doesn't already have one. Not ready to lead a branch? You can still support us by spreading the word, sharing our mission, or connecting us with friends who might want to get involved. We also welcome donations and sponsorships. SPARK is a nonprofit, and your financial support helps us reach more students around the world.
